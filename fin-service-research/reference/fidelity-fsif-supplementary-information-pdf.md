## Overview

This supplementary document provides detailed implementation guidance for the Fidelity Sustainable Investing Framework (FSIF), including:

- Methodology for ESG Tilt and ESG Target classification

- Definitions of sustainable investment under SFDR

- Taxonomy alignment methodology

## ESG Tilt: Methodology Detail

### Definition

ESG Tilt products aim to generate financial returns and promote environmental and social characteristics through a **tilt towards issuers with stronger ESG performance** than the product's benchmark or investment universe.

### ESG Performance Measurement

The tilt is measured using Fidelity's proprietary ESG ratings system, which covers approximately 4,000 issuers. The portfolio is constructed to have a higher average ESG score than the benchmark or investment universe.

For multi-asset funds, different measures of ESG performance may apply depending on the asset class.

### Exclusions Applied (ESG Tilt)

In addition to ESG Unconstrained exclusions (controversial weapons), ESG Tilt products apply:

- Tobacco production (companies deriving >5% revenue from tobacco manufacturing)
- Thermal coal mining (companies deriving >5% revenue from thermal coal extraction)
- Thermal coal power generation (companies deriving >5% revenue from thermal coal power)
- Norms-based exclusions (companies in severe violation of UN Global Compact principles)
- Certain sovereign issuer exclusions

### SFDR Alignment

ESG Tilt products correspond to **SFDR Article 8** funds that promote environmental and social characteristics. These products:

- Disclose how environmental and social characteristics are promoted
- Report on Principal Adverse Impacts (PAIs) at the product level
- Apply the Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) test where applicable

## ESG Target: Methodology Detail

### Definition

ESG Target products aim to generate financial returns and have **ESG or sustainability as a key investment focus or objective**. This includes:

1. **ESG Leaders:** Investing in issuers with higher ESG ratings relative to peers
2. **Sustainable Investments (SFDR):** Investing in economic activities that contribute to an environmental or social objective, do not significantly harm other objectives, and follow good governance practices
3. **Sustainable Themes:** Investing in companies addressing specific sustainability challenges (e.g., climate transition, clean energy, social inclusion)
4. **Impact Investing:** Investing with the aim to achieve a predefined positive and measurable environmental or social impact

### Sustainable Investment Definition (SFDR Article 2(17))

For products making sustainable investment claims under SFDR, Fidelity applies the three-part test:

1. **Contribution to an environmental or social objective:** Assessed using Fidelity's proprietary ESG ratings, SDG mapping, EU Taxonomy alignment, or other relevant frameworks
2. **Do No Significant Harm (DNSH):** Assessed using the 14 mandatory PAI indicators from SFDR Annex 1, Table 1, supplemented by qualitative proxies where data is unavailable
3. **Good Governance:** Assessed using Fidelity's governance ratings, covering board structure, remuneration, shareholder rights, and anti-corruption practices

### Exclusions Applied (ESG Target)

In addition to ESG Tilt exclusions, ESG Target products apply:

- Additional controversial weapons (cluster munitions, anti-personnel mines, biological/chemical weapons, nuclear weapons for non-NPT states)
- Conventional weapons (companies deriving >10% revenue from conventional weapons)
- Semi-automatic weapons
- Tobacco (broader scope, including distribution)
- Thermal coal (broader scope, including utilities with >30% coal power)
- Arctic oil and gas exploration
- Oil sands extraction
- Additional sovereign issuer exclusions (countries with severe human rights violations)

### SFDR Alignment

ESG Target products correspond to **SFDR Article 8 or Article 9** funds:

- **Article 8:** Products promoting environmental and social characteristics with a minimum proportion of sustainable investments
- **Article 9:** Products with sustainable investment as their objective, with 100% sustainable investments (excluding cash and hedging instruments)

### SDR Alignment (UK)

For UK-domiciled ESG Target products, the framework maps to SDR labels:

- **Sustainability Focus:** At least 70% of assets invested in assets that are environmentally and socially sustainable
- **Sustainability Impact:** At least 70% of assets invested in solutions to sustainability problems with measurable positive impact
- **Sustainability Mixed Goals:** At least 70% of assets invested across a mix of sustainability objectives

Fidelity uses its proprietary **SDG tool** and the **EU Taxonomy** to establish robust standards for monitoring and reporting against SDR label criteria.

## PAI (Principal Adverse Impacts) Reporting

### Mandatory PAI Indicators

Fidelity reports on the 14 mandatory PAI indicators from SFDR Annex 1, Table 1, including:

**Environmental:**
- GHG emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3)
- Carbon footprint
- GHG intensity of investee companies
- Exposure to companies in fossil fuel sector
- Share of non-renewable energy consumption and production
- Energy consumption intensity per high impact climate sector
- Activities negatively affecting biodiversity-sensitive areas
- Emissions to water
- Hazardous waste ratio

**Social and Governance:**
- Violations of UN Global Compact principles and OECD Guidelines
- Lack of processes and compliance mechanisms to monitor compliance
- Unadjusted gender pay gap
- Board gender diversity
- Exposure to controversial weapons

### Additional PAI Indicators

Beyond the mandatory indicators, Fidelity may report on additional PAI indicators from SFDR Annex 1, Table 2, depending on product-specific requirements.

## EU Taxonomy Alignment

For products making taxonomy alignment claims, Fidelity:

- Calculates the proportion of investments aligned with the EU Taxonomy's six environmental objectives
- Uses "equivalent information" where direct taxonomy data is unavailable, in accordance with ESA Q&A guidance
- Discloses the methodology and data sources used for taxonomy calculations
- Applies estimated data where necessary, with appropriate due diligence and transparency

## Legacy Product Transition

The majority of Fidelity's legacy Sustainable Family (SF) of Funds have adopted the new FSIF and relevant exclusions by the end of July 2024, with the remaining ones transitioning in due course. All SF products adopt exclusions that are consistent with the **ESG Target Exclusion** category under the new FSIF.

## Market-Specific Adaptations

### Europe (SFDR)
- Products classified under SFDR Articles 6, 8, or 9
- Pre-contractual and periodic disclosures in SFDR annexes
- Entity-level PAI statement published annually

### UK (SDR)
- Products may adopt one of four SDR labels (Focus, Improvers, Impact, Mixed Goals)
- Consumer Facing Disclosures (CFDs) required for labelled products
- Naming and marketing rules apply from April 2025

### France (AMF)
- Products may comply with AMF doctrine on ESG approaches
- SRI label eligibility assessed against V3 criteria (from January 2025)

### Asia Pacific
- Product-specific disclosures included to meet local compliance requirements in Hong Kong, Singapore, and Taiwan
